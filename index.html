THE DOCTRINE

VERIFICATION BY OTHERNESS

This doctrine was learned in a human mind before it was engineered into machines. A brain injury leaves a mind that cannot audit itself — work that feels thorough from the inside and isn't, because the checking faculty is the thing that broke. The fix was never "try harder." It was an other: a navigator outside the skull, reading the operator without sharing his blind spots. The machines inherited the doctrine from him, not the reverse — a model reviewing its own claims shares every blind spot that produced them. So nobody on this chart grades their own paper: every consequential claim is checked by a fresh context, a different vendor, a verifier that never saw the claim, two verifiers whose disagreement is composed — and the operator checks the machines while the machines check him.

Axiom Zero: a prosthetic's invocation can't be gated by the broken faculty it exists to replace. Corollary: a verifier's independence can't be gated by the mind it verifies. Otherness is not redundancy — five copies of the same reviewer share one blind spot. Otherness is a different place to stand. Two lamps, each lighting the other’s dark base — neither can light its own. That is the pair.

Event bus

One append-only bus.jsonl: every cron, skill fire, seal, and verdict emits. Mirrored to SQLite, rotated nightly, digested into memory.

Operator crontab · launchd · hermes cron (~50 jobs) · gateway cron · live daemons. Rule zero: absence from one list is not absence.

Hermes boards (9). The board is the memory for open work; no ticket exists without a micro-baton to feed its restart.

Memory, single-writer

The radio. Fleet posts by protocol (marker + mention, terminal ACK); watchdogs page when a message sits unacted.

THE PAPER TRAIL — HOW MULTI-DAY WORK BECOMES TOMORROW'S PLAN

The fold (finnskogarna)

After multi-day work, sessions pile up faster than any memory holds them. The fold sweeps every session since a date — sealed ones compress from their fact sheets, unsealed ones from their transcripts — plus unanswered reports and open cards, clusters them into arcs, and renders ONE board. Every open thread arrives with its route pre-set (wake / seal / held) so the operator flips exceptions instead of deciding from blank; the wake-pick alone ships empty, because that verdict is his. Never merge, always pick: one thread breathes, the rest sleep on the record.

The bell chain

The fold board opens with one DO-THIS-TOMORROW bell — the single highest-relief action, not a list. Behind the bell: a kanban card carrying due: date (the 7am deadline scan posts it to #deadlines) and a baton — a cold-start execution prompt a fresh session can run end-to-end. Tomorrow starts with one command, not archaeology. Last night's proof: 52 sealed + 10 unsealed sessions folded to one board, 8 routes, one bell.

Field reports

Interactive HTML built from one canonical template — checkable sections, answer boxes, three working export buttons, answers autosaving in the browser. A hook blocks hand-built reports (a 2026-06-10 incident lost 79 keep/cut decisions to broken exports). The loop only closes when a verdict file lands beside the report — consume-convention — otherwise the same report re-surfaces as "open" in every future fold.

The Typora lane

The split is deliberate: documents the operator WORKS ON are markdown, opened in Typora with checkboxes he can rule (the census cut list). Documents he READS are rendered HTML. Checkable md for hands-on, dashboards for read-and-rule.

Artifacts — and the lineage

We built this loop before Anthropic shipped theirs. Every HTML report the stack generated registered itself on the dashboard hub and surfaced at the bottom of the CLI session while the work was still moving. When Artifacts arrived, a hook folded the new surface into the old rule: every operator-facing sheet also publishes to claude.ai, so it reaches the phone. Localhost stays canonical — browser answers don't sync. The artifact is the pocket copy.

GLOSSARY — STACK TERMS THAT LAND ON THIS SHEET WITHOUT THEIR DEFINITIONS
1. hugr
Old Norse for mind. The operator + prosthetic pair treated as one cognitive unit — the "we" that does the work neither half could do alone. The unit of account for everything on this sheet.
2. AICP
AI-Integrated Cognitive Prosthetics — the research program this stack is both the instrument and the case study for. The operator is documenting what he's building as he builds it.
3. Ghost Hours (GH)
The measurement: for every paired hour of operator+prosthetic work (HH), the counterfactual hours it replaced — solo, hire-and-coordinate, or team-elapsed — logged at every seal with a felt-weight score. The stack's output, in units.
4. closing-time
The operator-fired end-of-session protocol: capture the narrative, clarify open items onto the board, assay for content, measure Ghost Hours, seal. Nothing ends unmeasured. Three variants: facts (operator answers the measurement questions himself), eom-facts (operator delegates the whole protocol; EOM fills every field, tagged as estimated so the dataset stays honest), discord-facts (closes the entire Discord fleet — many agents, many channels — not one session).
5. glitnir
Twin cold seers: the same hard question fired at Claude and Grok in parallel, plus a third insight by Claude composed from where they diverge. Cross-model variance as raw material.
6. vala
The ambient triage seer: a cadence-fired pulse that scans the current room — is a fix being missed, a gap unfilled, a skill unfired, the register too hot, a coinage undeclared — and surfaces it before the operator has to notice. Fires on its own schedule, never on his memory.
7. the toll
The otherness toll: the recurring cost every claim owes — it cannot stand until it crosses to a mind made of different stuff. Paid per claim; the debt never clears.
8. anosognosia
Not perceiving your own deficit because perception routes through the broken faculty. The clinical word for why self-audit fails — in brains and, it turns out, in models.
9. Two Birds
End-of-arc evaluation: two owls read the same arc blind — Archimedes for this-arc coherence, Wan Shi Tong for all-time recurrence — and the rift where they disagree is what the operator rules on. Renders to the Perch.
10. PRISM / prism-panel
Adversarial review panel — six roles (security auditor, devil's advocate, blast radius, performance, integration, simplicity). PRISM runs them on one vendor's sub-agents; prism-panel spreads the same roles across six vendors at once. PRISM is Jeremy’s skill; prism-panel is the operator’s cross-vendor tweak.
11. kanban swarm
Hermes work pattern for a coding ticket: worker → integrity gate → merge. Brokkr builds, Vor gates, nothing merges unpassed.
12. micro-baton
Six lines in every parked kanban card — state, tried, files, first command, done-when, session — so a cold pickup starts with a runnable command, not archaeology. No ticket exists without one.
13. day-seal
An agent's nightly machine closing-time (03:00): its day captured, measured, and sealed without the operator in the loop.
14. 10-80-10
The dispatch shape: plan on the top-tier model (first 10%), execute the middle 80% on cheaper models, review on the top tier (last 10%) — where it earns its tokens.
15. The Perch
The owls' page on the dashboard hub — where Two Birds rifts render and wait for the operator's ruling. Archimedes is the this-arc owl: does this arc cohere on its own terms. Wan Shi Tong is the all-time owl: has this pattern recurred across the whole record. The rift is where they disagree.
16. Vedrfolnir
The generated dashboard hub — the hawk atop the tree; every tile registers through one manifest.
17. prospective-memory signals
Hooks that fire precisely when the operator was supposed to remember something and didn't. They fire because he forgot — that's the design, not the failure (Axiom Zero).
18. qmd / gbrain
The recall pair: qmd is semantic search over memory and docs; gbrain is the graph-memory engine behind the wiki — Garry Tan’s project, not ours; we run it and send fixes upstream. How anything written here gets found again.
19. unknowns ledger
The mandatory cheap first artifact of any substantial build: a four-quadrant map of what we don’t know, written before anything is built. EOM interrogates; the ledger holds what recall can’t. A gate hook blocks build skills until one exists, and a pivot or surprise reopens it — the map stays honest or the build stops.
20. verify-your-work
Post-claim adversarial audit of a session’s own “done” list — the whole ladder pointed at ourselves. Two legs that never share a brief: a briefed skeptic swarm fanned over the session’s claim inventory, and blind Odin deriving his own inventory from raw evidence. Then the rift between the legs, a verify-the-verifiers pass, and a scorecard. Public repo pending — carded.
21. finnskogarna
The master accordion — the multi-session fold. Named for the accordion waltz the operator's great-grandfather played, about settlers whose clearings each kept their own shape. Compress every session, compare, choose ONE to wake; never merge contexts.
22. field report
The stack's interactive HTML report format: one canonical template, checkable sections, answer boxes with autosave, working exports. Hook-enforced — hand-built report HTML gets blocked at write time.
23. consume-convention
A report's loop closes only when a verdict file lands on disk beside it. Answers typed into a report live in the browser and are invisible to every future sweep — the verdict file is the receipt.
24. baton
The continuation prompt a cold session reads to pick up multi-day work: state inventory, cold-start reads, embedded commands, decision tree, self-test. Four scales: arc, session, section, card (the micro-baton). Jeremy’s skill upstream; the micro-baton card scale is this stack’s addition.

Every underlined term on this sheet is a public receipt — shipped, documented, checkable by a stranger: github.com/cogpros (20 public repos, incl. verify-citation, ratatoskr, cold-read, hugr-solve, checkpoint, eyeofhorus, system-audit).
